Githeri Recipe

Description

Githeri is a traditional Kenyan one-pot dish made by boiling maize and beans together, then often sautéed with onions, tomatoes,
and spices to create a hearty, nutritious meal. Originally rooted in Kikuyu cuisine, it’s widely loved across Kenya for its simplicity,
affordability, and filling nature. Githeri can be enjoyed plain, stewed, or fried, and is sometimes paired with vegetables, potatoes, or avocado for extra flavor.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups boiled maize
  • 2 cups boiled beans (red kidney beans or yellow beans)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped or blended
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1-2 carrots, diced (optional)
  • 1 potato, diced (optional)
  • garam masala (optional)
  • Table salt
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Fresh coriander (dhania), chopped

Steps

  1. If using dry beans/maize, soak overnight and boil until soft.
  2. In a pot, heat oil. Add chopped onions and sauté until soft and golden.
  3. Add garlic and sauté till fragrant
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until soft and saucy.
  5. Add garam masala and other spices of choice and stir.
  6. Add carrots, potatoes, and a little water. Simmer until almost cooked.
  7. Add in the cooked maize and beans. Mix well.
  8. Add a bit more water if needed for stew consistency and salt to taste
  9. Simmer for 10-15 minutes to blend flavors.
  10. Turn off the heat and sprinkle chopped coriander before serving.

Serving

Serve with a side of avocado and or hot cocoa
