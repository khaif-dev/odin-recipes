Pumpkin Chapati Recipe

Description

Chapati is a soft, unleavened flatbread commonly made in East Africa and South Asia. It’s made by mixing wheat flour, water,
and a bit of salt or sugar into a soft dough, which is then rolled out into thin circles and cooked on a hot pan with a light
brushing of oil/ghee. The result is a golden, slightly puffed, tender bread perfect for scooping up stews or veggies.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • cup mashed pumpkin
  • 1-2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 tablespoon oil or melted butter/ghee
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1-2 tbsp sugar
  • Warm water

Steps

  1. Peel, chop, and boil/steam pumpkin until soft. Let it cool then Mash/ blend for extra smoothness.
  2. In a large bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar.
  3. Mix the mashed pumpkin with oil or melted butter/ghee in a separate bowl.
  4. Add the wet ingredients slowly into the dry ingredients as you mix to form a soft dough
  5. If too dry, add water a tablespoon at a time.If too sticky, add a bit more flour.
  6. Knead for about 5-8 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  7. Cover and let it rest for 20-30 minutes.
  8. Divide dough into equal balls and roll them out into a thin circle while brushing them lightly with oil or ghee.
  9. Sprinkle a little flour evenly on top of the circle and roll it up into a log, then coil it into a spiral.
  10. Let the coils rest for 5-10 minutes.
  11. Roll each coil gently into a round chapati again.
  12. Heat a pan over medium heat and cook each chapati 30-45 seconds on one side then flip.
  13. Brush with a little oil or ghee and press gently with a spatula. Flip again and repeat until both sides are golden and cooked through.
  14. Repeat the remaining dough
  15. Keep Chapati covered to maintain the softness

Serving

Serve with Stew/beverage of choice.
